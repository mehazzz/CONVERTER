METHODS FOR CONVERSIONS

Binary to decimal conversion

To convert a binary number to its decimal equuivalent:

  1. Start with the given binary number.
  2. Write down the binary digits from right to left, assigning powers of 2 to each digit position.
  3. Evaluate the powers of 2 for each digit position.
  4. Multiply the respective digit by its corresponding power of 2.
  5. Sum up the results obtained in the previous step to get the decimal equivalent.

Octal to decimal conversion

To convert a octal number to its decimal equivalent:

  1. Start with the given octal number.
  2. Write down the octal digits from right to left, assigning powers of 8 to each digit position.
  3. Evaluate the powers of 8 for each digit position.
  4. Multiply the respective digit by its corresponding power of 8.
  5. Sum up the results obtained in the previous step to get the decimal equivalent.

Example: Octal number: 67

(6 * 8^1) + (7 * 8^0).

8^1 = 8 and 8^0 = 1.


(6 * 8) + (7 * 1) = 48 + 7 = 55.

Decimal number: 55

Hexadecimal to decimal conversion

To convert a Hexadecimal number to its decimal equivalent:

  1. Start with the given hexadecimal number.
  2. Write down the hexadecimal digits from right to left, assigning powers of 16 to each digit position.
  3. Convert any hexadecimal letters (A-F) to their decimal equivalents.
  4. Evaluate the powers of 16 for each digit position
  5. Multiply the respective digit (including converted letters) by its corresponding power of 16.

Example: Hexadecimal number 2B6

(2 * 16^2) + (B * 16^1) + (6 * 16^0).

16^2 = 256, 16^1 = 16, and 16^0 = 1.


(2 * 256) + (11 * 16) + (6 * 1) = 512 + 176 + 6 = 694.

Decimal number: 694
