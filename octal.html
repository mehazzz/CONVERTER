METHODS FOR CONVERSIONS

Binary to octal conversion

To convert a binary number to its octal equuivalent:

  1. Start with the given binary number.
  2. Group the binary digits into sets of three digits, starting from the right.
  3. If necessary, pad the leftmost group with zeros to make it a set of three digits. In this case, no padding is required as the number of digits is already a multiple of three.
  4. Convert each group of three binary digits to its octal equivalent.
  5. Combine the octal equivalents obtained from each group to get the final octal number.

Example: Binary number 101110110

101 110 110

101 in binary is 5 in octal.
110 in binary is 6 in octal.
110 in binary is 6 in octal.
101110110 in octal is 566

Octal number: 566

Decimal to octal conversion

To convert a decimal number to its octal equuivalent:

  1. Start with the given decimal number.
  2. Divide the decimal number by 8.
  3. Write down the remainder as the least significant digit (LSB) of the octal number.
  4. Continue dividing the quotient obtained in the previous step by 8 until the quotient becomes zero.
  5. Write down each remainder obtained from the divisions as subsequent digits of the octal number, from bottom to top.

Hexadecimal to octal conversion

To convert a hexadecimal number to its octal equuivalent:

  1. Start with the given hexadecimal number.
  2. Convert the hexadecimal number to its binary equivalent.
  3. Group the binary digits into sets of three from the right.
  4. If necessary, pad the leftmost group with zeros to make it a set of three digits. In this case, padding is not required.
  5. Convert each group of three binary digits to its octal equivalent.
  6. Combine the octal equivalents obtained from each group to get the final octal number.

Example: Hexadecimal number 2AC

100 in binary is 4 in octal.
